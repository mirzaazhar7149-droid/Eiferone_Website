04 Legal Basis for Processing

  • Contract: Processing necessary to perform a contract or take pre-contractual steps at your request.
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing necessary for our legitimate business interests, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms.
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required to comply with a legal obligation, including financial record-keeping under UK company and tax law.
  • Consent: Where we have obtained your explicit consent, which you may withdraw at any time without affecting prior processing.

Where we process sensitive client engagement data (vulnerability findings, infrastructure maps, Proof of Concept evidence), the legal basis is Contract — that data exists solely to fulfil the agreed engagement and is subject to the strict retention and destruction schedule in Section 06.

  • Service Providers: Trusted third-party tools (e.g., form processing, email hosting) acting as data processors under written contract. These processors are contractually prohibited from using your data for any purpose other than providing their service to us.

Client Engagement Data — Strict Isolation Vulnerability findings, Proof of Concept evidence, infrastructure maps, credentials, and all technical assessment data are never transmitted to, stored in, or processed by third-party SaaS platforms (including cloud document editors, CRMs, or project management tools). This data is held exclusively in access-controlled, encrypted internal storage and governed by your signed engagement agreement and NDA.

07 Your Rights

  • Right of Access (Article 15): Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification (Article 16): Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure (Article 17): Request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18): Ask us to limit how we use your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to Data Portability (Article 20): Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object (Article 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent (Article 7): Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent — withdrawal does not affect prior lawful processing.
Right to Erasure — Legal Retention Carve-Out While we will honour erasure requests to the fullest extent possible, certain data cannot be deleted where retention is required by law. Financial records, signed contracts, and statements of work must be retained for 6 years under the Limitation Act 1980 and UK tax law, regardless of an erasure request. Where this applies, we will inform you in writing, delete everything that can be deleted, and restrict all further processing of any retained records strictly to legal compliance purposes only — no other use will be made of that data.

To exercise any right, email [email protected]. We respond within 30 calendar days. If you believe we have handled your data unlawfully, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint.

09 Security of Processing

As a cybersecurity consultancy, we hold ourselves to a materially higher standard than a generic privacy policy requires. The following technical and organisational measures are applied to all personal data and client engagement data we hold:

  • Encryption in transit: All data transferred to and from our systems uses TLS 1.2 or higher. Unencrypted connections (plain HTTP) are rejected at the server level.
  • Encryption at rest: All client engagement data and personal data is stored encrypted at rest using AES-256 or equivalent. Unencrypted storage of sensitive data is prohibited by internal policy.
  • Access controls: Least-privilege access is enforced across all internal systems. Client engagement data is accessible only to the specific personnel working on that engagement — not to the broader team.
  • Secure destruction: Data destruction follows NIST SP 800-88 guidelines (or DoD 5220.22-M equivalent for digital media). Written confirmation of destruction is available on request for all engagement data.
  • Internal security reviews: We conduct regular reviews of our own infrastructure — we do not exempt ourselves from the standards we apply to clients.
  • Third-party processor controls: Any processor handling personal data on our behalf is required to maintain equivalent security standards under a written data processing agreement.
  • Incident response: We maintain a documented breach response procedure. In the event of a confirmed breach, internal escalation begins immediately.
Breach Notification In the event of a personal data breach likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by Article 33 of UK GDPR. You will receive clear information about what data was affected, the likely consequences, and the steps we have taken.
