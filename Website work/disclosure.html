01 Introduction

We take security seriouslyAs a cybersecurity company, we hold ourselves to a high standard. If you've found a vulnerability in our systems, we want to hear from you.

EiferOne Ltd is committed to the security of our own systems. We recognise that security researchers play a vital role in identifying vulnerabilities that may have been missed. This policy sets out how to safely report security vulnerabilities in EiferOne's own systems.

02 Our Commitment to You

  • Acknowledging your report within 48 hours of receipt.
  • Providing an initial assessment within 5 business days.
  • Keeping you informed throughout the remediation process.
  • Not taking legal action against researchers acting in good faith within this policy.
  • Crediting you publicly (with permission) once the issue is resolved.

03 Scope

This policy applies only to EiferOne's own assets — not client systems or third-party services.

✓ In Scope

  • eiferone.com (main website)
  • Any subdomains of eiferone.com
  • EiferOne web applications
  • EiferOne email infrastructure
  • Authentication mechanisms
  • API endpoints

– Out of Scope

  • Client systems or infrastructure
  • Third-party services we use
  • Social engineering attacks
  • Physical security attacks
  • Denial of service (DoS/DDoS)
  • Spam or phishing campaigns

04 How to Report

Security Contactsecurity@eiferone.com

For sensitive reports, you may request our PGP public key to encrypt your submission.

Please include: a clear description of the vulnerability, the affected URL or component, step-by-step reproduction instructions, screenshots or proof-of-concept (where safe), your severity assessment, and any CVE references.

05 Disclosure Process

01

Acknowledgement — within 48 hours

We confirm receipt and assign a tracking reference.

02

Triage — within 5 business days

We reproduce, assess, and communicate our initial severity rating to you.

03

Remediation — varies by severity

Critical/High issues are prioritised immediately. We keep you updated throughout.

04

Verification — after fix deployed

We notify you when the fix is live and invite you to verify.

05

Public Disclosure — by mutual agreement

With permission, we publish a security advisory crediting your discovery. We aim to coordinate within 90 days.

06 Safe Harbour

EiferOne will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

  • Act in good faith and report vulnerabilities promptly without exploiting them.
  • Avoid accessing or modifying data beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability.
  • Do not perform testing that degrades system availability.
  • Do not disclose publicly before we have had a reasonable opportunity to remediate.
⚠ Please NoteSafe harbour applies to good-faith researchers within this policy. EiferOne reserves the right to pursue legal remedies against individuals who deliberately cause harm or act outside this policy.

07 Exclusions

– Out of Scope Reports
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party services not controlled by EiferOne
  • Theoretical vulnerabilities without a working proof of concept
  • Issues requiring physical access to a user's device
  • Self-XSS or implausible real-world user interaction
  • Clickjacking on pages with no sensitive actions
  • Missing headers without demonstrated impact
  • Reports generated by automated tools without manual validation
  • Previously known or already-reported vulnerabilities
  • Denial of service attacks

08 Recognition

While EiferOne does not currently operate a formal bug bounty programme, for valid in-scope reports we will:

  • Credit you by name or handle on our acknowledgements page (with permission).
  • Provide a written letter of acknowledgement if requested.
  • Give serious consideration to recognition for high-impact findings.

We are evaluating establishing a formal bug bounty programme and will update this policy when launched.
